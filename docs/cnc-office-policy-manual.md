# CnC Realty — Office Policy Manual

*This manual explains how things actually work day-to-day at CnC Realty. It is incorporated by reference into your Independent Contractor Agreement ("ICA") and referenced throughout it — most directly in Section 3.2 (general compliance), Section 3.7 (Supervision of Licensed Activities), Section 10 (Transaction Management & Activity Reporting), and Section 24 (Harassment and Discrimination Policy). Where the ICA sets the binding legal terms, this manual explains the practical steps for following them. Broker may update this manual from time to time, with or without prior notice, as described in Section 26 of the ICA.*

---

## 1. Introduction

CnC Realty is a cloud-based brokerage — there is no physical office, and Associate-Licensees work from wherever suits them. Everything that would traditionally happen in an office (file review, checklist compliance, document submission, deadline tracking) happens through the CnC Realty CRM platform instead. This manual is your reference for how those processes actually work.

If anything in this manual conflicts with the ICA, the ICA controls.

---

## 2. Supervision of Licensed Activities

Under Section 3.7 of the ICA, Broker supervises Associate-Licensee's licensed activities. In practice, that supervision happens through the CnC Realty platform, not in-person oversight:

- Every transaction and listing file is reviewed against a broker-defined document checklist before commission is released.
- Broker (or a designated Transaction Coordinator) reviews uploaded documents and either **certifies the file as complete** or **rejects an item with a note** explaining what's missing or needs correction.
- Written-approval requirements (personal/family property sales, dual agency, short sale negotiations — see ICA Section 6) are requested and granted through the platform.
- The admin dashboard gives Broker visibility into every open file across the brokerage, including upcoming deadlines, so supervision is continuous rather than a one-time check at closing.

This supervision is limited to licensed-activity compliance. It does not extend to your hours, schedule, or the day-to-day methods you use to find and work with clients — that stays entirely up to you, consistent with your independent contractor status.

## 3. Transaction Management System

**Opening a file.** As soon as you have an accepted purchase agreement, executed listing agreement, or any other reportable real estate activity (see ICA Section 10.1 for the full list), create the corresponding transaction or listing file in the CnC Realty platform. Do this within 48 hours of the activity occurring.

**Dual agency.** If you represent both sides of a transaction, you must create **two separate transaction records** — one for the listing/seller side, one for the buyer side — per ICA Section 10.2.

**Uploading documents.** Each file has a checklist assigned automatically based on file type (e.g., CA Purchase — Buyer Side, CA Purchase — Seller Side, CA Lease — Tenant Side). Upload each required document to its corresponding checklist item as you receive it — don't wait until closing to upload everything at once.

**Getting paid.** Commission is not released until:
1. The transaction file is complete in the platform, and
2. Broker (or your assigned TC) has certified the file.

Closing a transaction without an open, certified file is grounds for withholding commission until the deficiency is corrected (ICA Section 10.3). If you want to receive commission directly from escrow, your file must be submitted for review at least two business days before close of escrow (ICA Section 7.3).

## 4. Working Remotely

There's no office to check in at. A few things make remote work function well for everyone:

- Keep your contact information (phone, email) current in your CnC Realty profile.
- Be reachable by phone and email, and aim to respond to client and broker inquiries within 24 hours.
- Store your transaction documents securely (the CnC platform handles this for anything uploaded to a file) and be able to produce them within 24 hours of a request from Broker.
- You're free to work from home, a personal office, or anywhere else — CnC does not dictate your schedule, hours, or working location.

## 5. Trust Fund / Earnest Money Handling

Per ICA Section 12, trust funds must go into a neutral escrow depository (an escrow or title company) — never into your personal or business accounts, and never as cash. In practice:

- Direct your buyer to deliver earnest money deposits directly to the escrow or title company, not to you.
- Never accept a check, money order, or cash from a client for a deposit, inspection, appraisal, or any other service.
- If a client tries to hand you funds of any kind, redirect them to the escrow company and notify Broker.

**Wire fraud advisory.** Never let a title company, escrow company, or attorney send *you* wiring instructions to forward to a client. Those instructions must go directly from the escrow/title company to the client. If you suspect a fraudulent or cyber-security incident involving a client transaction, notify Broker within 24 hours (ICA Section 18).

## 6. Marketing & Advertising Approval

All advertising and marketing materials — signage, listing photography captions, social media posts about a listing, flyers, whatever it is — need Broker's prior written approval before you use them, per ICA Section 14. Submit materials for approval through the platform or by contacting Broker directly.

You may not make telephone solicitations to anyone registered on a national or state do-not-call registry.

---

## 7. MLS / Board of REALTORS® Membership

CnC Realty does not select or pay for your local MLS or Board of REALTORS® membership — that's an individual responsibility (ICA Section 5), the same as it is at every brokerage. You are expected to:

- Join the Board of REALTORS® / MLS association that covers the area(s) you work in.
- Keep your membership and any required orientation/training current.
- Pay your own membership dues and MLS fees on time.

If you're unsure which association covers your market, ask Broker — Broker is a member of the applicable association(s) as your Designated REALTOR® and can point you in the right direction.

## 8. Document Checklist & Compliance Review

Checklist templates are set by Broker (currently: CA Purchase — Buyer Side, CA Purchase — Seller Side, CA Lease — Tenant Side, with more added as needed) and auto-apply the moment you open a new transaction or listing file. Each checklist item goes through one of three states:

- **Pending** — not yet uploaded.
- **In Review** — uploaded, awaiting Broker/TC review.
- **Approved** or **Rejected (with note)** — Broker/TC has reviewed it; a rejection always comes with an explanation of what needs to be fixed.

A file is only "certified complete" once every required checklist item is approved.

---

## 9. Personal & Family Property Transactions

Per ICA Section 6, you need Broker's written approval before representing yourself in the sale of your own property, a family member's property, or any property in which you hold an ownership interest. When this happens:

- Get written approval from Broker *before* the listing or offer is submitted.
- Open a transaction file in the platform like any other deal — CnC's fee structure applies the same way (see ICA Section 7.4 on dual-agency and agent-relative-sale fee doubling).
- Be prepared for closer file review, since these transactions carry more scrutiny by nature.

## 10. Confidentiality & Data Practices

You'll have access to client data, transaction details, and other information through the CnC Realty platform that belongs to Broker and its clients (ICA Sections 3.7 and 20). Keep it confidential during and after your time with CnC — don't export client lists, transaction details, or platform data for use outside of CnC business, and don't share your platform login credentials with anyone.

When your association with CnC ends, you're required to return or destroy any confidential materials in your possession — files, client lists, marketing materials — within 24 hours of termination (ICA Section 20).

## 11. Harassment & Discrimination — Reporting Procedure

CnC Realty prohibits harassment and discrimination of any kind, as stated in ICA Section 24. If you experience or witness conduct that violates that policy:

1. Report it to Broker directly, as soon as possible, in writing if you're able.
2. Broker will investigate promptly — this may include speaking with you, the person the complaint is about, and any other relevant witnesses.
3. Your identity and the identity of the person the complaint concerns will be kept confidential to the extent possible during the investigation.
4. If the complaint is substantiated, Broker will take corrective action, which may include immediate termination of the other party's affiliation with CnC per ICA Section 24.

You will not face retaliation for reporting in good faith.

## 12. Personal Assistants

If you'd like to employ a personal assistant, ICA Section 23 requires:

- A written agreement between you and your assistant covering compensation, supervision, and compliance responsibilities.
- If your assistant holds a California real estate license, a copy of that license provided to Broker.

You remain responsible for supervising your assistant and making sure they follow the terms of your ICA.

---

## 13. Termination Procedures

If your affiliation with CnC Realty ends — whether you leave or the relationship is terminated — here's what happens:

- Active listings and pending transactions stay with CnC Realty unless you and Broker execute a written referral agreement transferring them to your new brokerage (ICA Section 21).
- You must immediately stop using CnC Realty's name, logo, and trademarks (ICA Section 21).
- You must return or destroy any confidential materials in your possession within 24 hours (ICA Section 20).
- Commission owed to you for transactions closed before your termination date is still paid out, so long as you're not in default of the ICA (ICA Section 7.10).
