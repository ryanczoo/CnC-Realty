## 7. COMPENSATION / BROKER FEES

**7.1. Compensation Structure.** Compensation shall be charged to the parties who enter into listing or buyer agreements for services requiring a real estate license. Associate-Licensee may use its own discretion regarding the commission fee to charge its clients, subject to the minimum fee described below. Broker's fee shall be deducted at closing before disbursement to Associate-Licensee.

**7.2. Flat Transaction Fee.** Broker's flat fee per closed transaction is **$990**, inclusive of Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance coverage as described in Section 9 for sale values up to and including $1,000,000. For transactions exceeding $1,000,000, an E&O Supplement is added to the base fee.

**E&O Supplement:** $400 per $500,000 (or fraction thereof) of sale price over $1,000,000.

| Sale Price | E&O Supplement | Total Broker Fee |
|--------------------|----------------|------------------|
| $0 – $1,000,000 | $0 | **$990** |
| $1,000,001 – $1,500,000 | $400 | **$1,390** |
| $1,500,001 – $2,000,000 | $800 | **$1,790** |
| $2,000,001 – $2,500,000 | $1,200 | **$2,190** |
| $2,500,001 – $3,000,000 | $1,600 | **$2,590** |
| $3,000,001 – $3,500,000 | $2,000 | **$2,990** |
| $3,500,001 – $4,000,000 | $2,400 | **$3,390** |
| $4,000,001+ | +$400 per additional $500k | $3,390 + supplement |

No monthly fee is charged.

**7.3. Associate-Licensee Commission.** After deduction of the flat transaction fee, 100% of the remaining commission is disbursed to Associate-Licensee. Associate-Licensee may receive commission directly from escrow, provided that: (a) the completed transaction file was submitted for Broker's review no later than two (2) business days before close of escrow, and (b) Broker has certified the file as complete. Unauthorized release of a commission check is grounds for immediate termination.

**7.4. Dual Agency and Agent-Relative Sales.** Any transaction in which Associate-Licensee represents both buyer and seller (or both landlord and tenant) is counted as two (2) separate transactions for fee purposes. The full transaction fee — including the base flat fee and any applicable E&O Supplement — shall be charged twice: once for the buyer-side representation and once for the seller-side representation.

The same double-fee structure applies to transactions involving the sale or purchase of property owned by or for the benefit of Associate-Licensee, Associate-Licensee's immediate family member, or any entity in which Associate-Licensee holds an ownership interest, where Associate-Licensee also acts as the representing agent. Written approval from Broker is required for all such transactions per Section 6.

**7.5. Outbound Referral Transactions.** When Associate-Licensee refers a client to another licensed real estate agent or brokerage and performs no further services on the resulting transaction, Broker's fee is ten percent (10%) of the referral commission Associate-Licensee receives, or $200, whichever is greater. This section applies only to outbound referrals. If Associate-Licensee instead receives a referral from another agent or brokerage and completes the transaction, the standard transaction fee described in Section 7.2 applies to that transaction.

**7.6. Minimum Fee.** If a transaction closes at a commission amount less than the applicable flat fee, Associate-Licensee is responsible for remitting the balance of Broker's fee to Broker at closing.

**7.7. Commission Advances.** Broker does not offer commission advances. All commissions are paid after close of escrow and file certification.

**7.8. Deductions.** Broker may deduct from Associate-Licensee's commission any actual and pending reasonable expenses arising from Associate-Licensee's activity, including but not limited to: legal expenses, MLS/board dues paid on Associate-Licensee's behalf, repayment of any advance, and judgments or settlements. This paragraph applies regardless of whether Associate-Licensee is at fault and whether the matter has been adjudicated.

**7.9. Broker-Provided Leads.** For any transaction that closes as a direct result of a lead provided by Broker, Associate-Licensee shall remit to Broker a referral fee equal to thirty percent (30%) of Associate-Licensee's gross commission on that transaction. The flat transaction fee described in Section 7.2 does not apply to Broker-provided leads. The referral fee shall be deducted from Associate-Licensee's commission at closing. Broker will identify leads as "Broker-provided" in writing at the time of referral.

**7.10. Post-Termination Compensation.** Upon termination of this Agreement, payments shall cease; provided that Associate-Licensee shall be entitled to commissions for transactions closed prior to the termination date for which Associate-Licensee has not yet been paid, so long as Associate-Licensee is not in default of this Agreement. Broker may deduct all financial obligations owed to Broker from any commissions due at termination. If pending transactions require further work normally rendered by Associate-Licensee, Broker may assign another licensee to complete the work, and reasonable compensation to the completing licensee shall be deducted from the terminating Associate-Licensee's commission share.

**7.11. Compensation Disputes.** If a dispute arises regarding compensation — including the amount owed, entitlement to a commission, or an allocation between Associate-Licensee and another agent, broker, or party to a transaction — Broker may hold the disputed compensation until the dispute is resolved. Broker shall incur no liability to Associate-Licensee for any delay in payment while a dispute is pending.

**7.12. Team and Co-Listing Transactions.** If two or more Associate-Licensees participate on the same side of a transaction (co-listing or co-selling), the transaction fee described in Section 7.2 is charged once per transaction side, and the resulting commission is distributed among the participating Associate-Licensees according to their written agreement. If the participating Associate-Licensees have not provided Broker with a signed written agreement allocating the commission, Broker may withhold the full commission until such an agreement is provided. Associate-Licensees operating as a team under a shared name shall each remain individually subject to the transaction fee, unless the team has formed a licensed corporate entity, in which case a single transaction fee applies to that entity.

**7.13. Multi-Parcel Transactions.** If a transaction includes multiple properties identified by separate assessor's parcel numbers (APNs), Broker may charge a separate transaction fee — including the base flat fee and any applicable E&O Supplement — for each APN included in the transaction, regardless of whether the closing, title, or escrow company combines the parcels into a single closing for convenience.

**7.14. No Direct Compensation.** Associate-Licensee shall not request, receive, or accept compensation directly from a client, another broker, or any other party for licensed real estate activity without Broker's prior written approval. All compensation must be processed through Broker in accordance with this Agreement.

**7.15. Broker-Assigned Reassignment.** If Broker determines that Associate-Licensee is mishandling a transaction, listing, or client relationship, Broker may assign a manager or another Associate-Licensee to assist with or complete the transaction. In such cases, the assisting party shall receive twenty-five percent (25%) of Associate-Licensee's commission on that transaction, with the balance remaining payable to Associate-Licensee. If a client requests a different agent, Broker may reassign the client to another Associate-Licensee within CnC Realty; in that event, the original Associate-Licensee shall be entitled only to a referral commission, if any, and shall cease further communication with the client regarding that transaction.

**7.16. Lease Transactions.** Broker's fee on lease and rental transactions is ten percent (10%) of Associate-Licensee's commission, or $200, whichever is greater. The flat transaction fee described in Section 7.2 does not apply to lease or rental transactions.

**8.2.** Use of the CnC TC Service is voluntary. Associate-Licensee may use any licensed TC of their choosing, or may self-coordinate, provided all required documentation is submitted to Broker's platform within required timelines.

In such cases, the $350 fee will be charged to the transaction regardless of client consent.

**8.4.** The CnC TC Service fee is separate from and in addition to the flat transaction fee described in Section 7.2.

## 9. ERRORS & OMISSIONS INSURANCE

**9.1.** Broker maintains an Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance policy covering real estate transactions brokered through CnC Realty Group. Coverage limits are $1,000,000 per claim and $1,000,000 aggregate. The flat transaction fee described in Section 7.2 includes E&O coverage for transactions with a sale value up to and including $1,000,000. For transactions exceeding $1,000,000, the E&O Supplement described in Section 7.2 covers the additional E&O exposure — no further E&O fee is charged beyond what is specified in the fee schedule.

**9.2.** Associate-Licensee is responsible for any deductible that may be assessed against a claim arising from Associate-Licensee's transaction(s). Broker's E&O policy does not cover: personal property transactions by Associate-Licensee (where Associate-Licensee is buying or selling their own property), prohibited activities listed in Section 6, or conduct found by a court or arbitration panel to constitute intentional fraud, misrepresentation, or gross negligence.

**9.3.** Associate-Licensee must receive written approval from Broker before filing any E&O claim. Unauthorized claims will result in Associate-Licensee being solely responsible for the deductible.

**10.3.** Commission will not be released until Associate-Licensee has created a complete transaction record in the CnC platform and Broker has certified the file as complete. ## 26. MENTORSHIP PROGRAM

**26.1.** CnC Realty Group offers an optional Mentorship Program for Associate-Licensees with little or no transactional experience. Participation in the Mentorship Program requires a separate written Mentorship Agreement signed by the Associate-Licensee, the assigned Mentor, and Broker.

**26.2.** Under the Mentorship Program, the commission split is **75% to Associate-Licensee and 25% to the assigned Mentor**. The flat transaction fee (Section 7.2), inclusive of any applicable E&O Supplement, and the CnC TC Service fee (Section 8, if applicable) are deducted from the gross commission **before** the 75/25 split is applied to the remaining commission.

**26.3.** The 75/25 mentorship split applies to **both sides** of any dual agency transaction. If Associate-Licensee represents both buyer and seller while enrolled in the Mentorship Program, the mentorship split applies to each side separately.

**26.4.** Upon successful completion of the Associate-Licensee's first closed transaction under the Mentorship Program, the Associate-Licensee will transition to 100% commission (less the applicable flat transaction fee) for all subsequent transactions, unless otherwise agreed in writing.
